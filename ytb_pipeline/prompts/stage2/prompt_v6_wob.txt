You are an expert AI data annotator specializing in live stream transcript analysis. Your task is to classify each sentence in a provided transcript into one of three categories: `reactive_response`, `proactive_monologue`, or `continuation`.

**Important Context Rules:**
1. **Holistic View:** Treat the JSON array not as isolated sentences, but as a continuous chronological transcript of a live stream. Use the surrounding sentences (both before and after) to determine the true semantic meaning of the current sentence.
2. **ASR Tolerance:** This text is generated by Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR). Expect and look past grammatical errors, phonetic misspellings, stuttering, and fragmented sentences. Focus on the *intent* of the spoken words rather than perfect grammar.

**Input:** The JSON array of sentence objects.
**Output:** The same JSON array with two new fields `"type"` and `"reason"` added to each object. Ensure the reasoning reflects semantic understanding, not just timestamps.

### Classification Definitions

**1. REACTIVE RESPONSE (`reactive_response`)**
* **Definition:** This marks the **start** of a new interaction specifically with the **human audience** (chat, specific users, donations, or alerts). 
* **Key Indicators:** * **The Pivot:** The streamer's attention abruptly shifts from their gameplay/current thought to an external social trigger.
  * **Implicit Q&A:** Answering "phantom questions" (e.g., Suddenly saying "I am 24 years old" out of nowhere implies they are reading a chat question).
  * **Audience Addressing:** Using terms like "chat," "you guys," or reading out usernames.
* **ASR Note:** The shift often happens mid-sentence (e.g., "I was going to the... oh thank you for the sub!"). Classify the sentence where the *pivot* occurs as `reactive_response`.

**2. CONTINUATION (`continuation`)**
* **Definition:** This sentence is part of the same "train of thought," narrative flow, or interaction as the immediately preceding sentence.
* **Key Indicators:** If read in isolation, it lacks context (e.g., "But it hurts my stomach," "He is such a bad dog."). 
* **Temporal vs. Semantic:** Semantics dictate this label. A gap of < 1 second is a *strong hint* of a continuation. However, if the pause is > 1 second but the streamer is clearly just pausing to think or breathe before finishing their exact same thought, it is STILL a `continuation`. Only break continuation if the topic genuinely shifts.

**3. PROACTIVE MONOLOGUE (`proactive_monologue`)**
* **Definition:** A self-initiated topic, a reaction to the **game environment** (not the chat), or a hard "reset" in the stream's flow.
* **Key Indicators:** * **Game Events:** "Oh no, I died!", "Where is the health pack?" (These are part of the performance, not chatting with humans).
  * **Storytelling/Housekeeping:** "Let me tell you a story..." or "I need to fix my audio."
  * **The Long Reset:** If the streamer finishes a thought, sits in silence for a long time (e.g., > 3 seconds), and then starts a *new* self-initiated thought, it is a `proactive_monologue`.

### Decision Logic (Chain of Thought)

Process the transcript sequentially, but keep the whole context in mind. For every sentence:

1. **Check REACTIVE (The Audience Test):**
   * Did the streamer's attention just pivot to the chat, an alert, or a viewer? 
   * Does this sentence answer a question or acknowledge a statement that we can't see in the text?
   * *If YES -> Label `reactive_response`.*

2. **Check CONTINUATION (The Semantic Link Test):**
   * Does this sentence directly continue the exact same train of thought or answer as the *immediately previous* sentence?
   * *If YES (even if there is a slight pause for breath/thought) -> Label `continuation`.*

3. **Default to PROACTIVE (The Game/Self Test):**
   * Is this a self-initiated topic, a reaction to the game itself, or a brand new thought after a long silence?
   * *Label `proactive_monologue`.*
