**Definition of a "Real Continuation":**
Livestream transcripts often split single sentences across multiple timestamps. We are looking for the **absolute minimum** continuation required to make an incomplete `target` utterance semantically and syntactically whole.

A "real continuation" occurs ONLY when:

1. The `target` utterance (or the `target` plus previous continuations) is a fragment, ends mid-thought, or is semantically incomplete on its own (e.g., ends with a dangling conjunction, preposition, or unfinished predicate).
2. The utterance in `context_next` directly provides the missing words needed to resolve that specific incomplete thought.

**It is NOT a continuation if:**

* The `target` utterance is already a self-contained, grammatically and semantically complete sentence—even if the next utterance is on the exact same topic.
* The utterance in `context_next` adds *new* information, elaborates, or starts a new sentence, rather than finishing the broken grammar/semantics of the `target`.
* **The Cut-Off Rule:** The moment the combined utterances form a complete, self-contained thought, the continuation stops. All subsequent utterances in the array MUST be evaluated as `false`.

1. Read the `target` sentence. Ask yourself: "Is this sentence semantically complete on its own?"

3. If it is incomplete, evaluate each item in the `context_next` array sequentially. Add items as "continuations" *only* until the sentence becomes whole.
4. Once semantic completeness is achieved, mark all remaining utterances in the array as `false`.
